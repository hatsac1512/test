Our Theory
of Change

A bold, community-driven framework for transforming global health outcomes — built on equity, co-creation, and the belief that every life has equal value.

March 2026
The Problem

The world has failed to deliver on its promises

Around the world, too many women, babies, children and young people die from preventable causes every day. Their health needs simply aren't prioritized.

Systemic biases, chronic under-investment, and policies that fail communities result in pain, permanent disability, trauma and even death for the most vulnerable.

In many countries, a lack of basic medical supplies and trained providers leaves millions without critical care that many take for granted.

Systemic biases and chronic under-investment leave women, children and adolescents behind in communities around the world.

Policy failures and lack of trained providers mean millions go without critical care that many take for granted.

GHA's role: walking alongside communities to remove barriers to health equity — from hyper-local to international.

Our Logic Model

From problem to lasting change

GHA's Theory of Change follows a clear, interconnected pathway — from acknowledging the problem, through our inputs and activities, all the way to the transformational outcomes we seek for communities globally.

01
Inputs
Acknowledge the Problem

Community-Led Engagement

Listening, co-creating, and building trusting relationships with locally-trusted health organizations and community champions.

02
Activities
Activities

Training & Capacity Building

Transformational leadership, health system strengthening, advocacy planning, and training the next generation of global health practitioners.

03
Outputs
Outputs

Strengthened Health Systems

Resilient community and health systems, evidence-based local solutions, and amplified voices of frontline workers at decision-making tables.

04
Outcomes
Outcomes

Health Equity for All

A healthier, more equitable future where every woman, child, and adolescent — everywhere — can access the care they deserve.

05
Impact
Impact

Transformational Change

Lasting systemic change that improves the health and well-being of women, children and adolescents — tackling global health inequities at scale.

What Will Bring About the Change

Six drivers of transformational change

GHA recognizes that what has been tried has not worked. Real, lasting change demands a fundamentally different approach.

01
Approach
Comprehensive & Long-Term

Change requires a comprehensive approach and a long-term commitment — not short-term interventions that fade without lasting roots.

02
Leadership
Community at Every Stage

Change warrants community leadership and meaningful involvement at every stage of design, delivery, and evaluation.

03
Systems
Interconnected Systems

The larger community and the formal health system are interconnected. Both must be strengthened simultaneously — one cannot succeed without the other.

04
Solutions
Local, Co-Designed Solutions

There is no one-size-fits-all solution. Local solutions must be co-designed with those most affected, honoring cultural and geographic nuances.

05
Collaboration
Collaboration for Sustainability

Sustainable change requires a comprehensive approach and long-term commitment — not short-term interventions that fade without lasting community roots.

06
Partnerships
Trust, Honesty & Accountability

Change calls for trusting partnerships built on honesty, transparency, and accountability — at every level, from community to international.

What We Do Everywhere and Always

Our non-negotiable commitments

These are the values and practices that guide every interaction, every partnership, and every program GHA undertakes, without exception.

🤝
Community-First

We value a community's ability to best know its own needs and priorities. We listen, and let that be the basis of our work.

🌱
Trust Local Expertise

We trust local leaders for expertise on cultural components of the work. No outsider knows a community better than its own people.

🔗
Co-Create Everything

We co-create, co-develop and collaborate at every stage. It is all shared work — no program belongs to GHA alone.

🧠
Mental Health is Health

We acknowledge and address mental health care as a crucial need — for the communities we serve, our partners, and ourselves.

🛡️
Safe Space for Transformation

We create a safe place to experience a transformation of thinking — for partners, for communities, and for our own team.

⚖️
Equity as a Lens

We apply a global health equity lens to everything — ensuring that the most marginalized are always centered in our work.

Our Mission

Driving transformational change to improve the health and well-being of women, children and adolescents by equipping communities, developing leaders, and strengthening health systems — wherever the need is greatest — while tackling global health inequities. Together, we are building a healthier, more equitable future.

01 — Introduction

Our commitment to your privacy

At Global Health Action ("GHA", "we", or "our"), we value your support and are committed to protecting your privacy. 02 — Information We Collect

Personal information we collect

When you visit our website, we may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Contact information, such as your name, home or other physical address, email address, telephone number, fax number, and/or organization name.
  • Unique identifiers and preference information such as your marketing preferences, internet protocol (IP) address, browser type, operating system, computer or mobile device, and your navigation actions on our website.

We may also collect personal information through our email communications. For instance, if your computer supports this functionality, we may receive a confirmation when you open an email we send you, or when you follow a hyperlink from one of our messages to our website.

03 — Tracking Technologies

Tracking technologies we use

We and our service providers use various technologies to collect information about visitors to our website. These technologies include cookies, web logging, beacons, tags, and scripts.

These tools are used to analyze trends, remember user settings, administer our website, track user movements around the website, and gather demographic information about our user base as a whole.

Cookie control. Users can control the use of cookies at the individual browser level. If you reject cookies, you may still use our website, but your ability to use some features or areas may be limited.

04 — How We Use Your Data

How we use the information collected

We may use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To improve the content and functionality of our website.
  • To provide personalized website content based on your preferences.
  • To communicate with you and respond to your inquiries and requests.
  • To send important notices, updates, and information about our work.
05 — How We Share Your Data

How we share personal information

GHA does not sell personal information about you to third parties. We will share your personal information with third parties only in the ways described in this Privacy Notice.

We may provide your personal information to third-party service providers that help us with our business activities — such as processing financial donations or offering customer service. These companies are authorized to use your personal information only as necessary to provide these services to us.

We may also disclose personal information in the following circumstances:

  • As required by law, such as to comply with a subpoena or other legal process.
  • When we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect our rights or property, or to protect your health and safety or the health and safety of others.
  • To investigate fraud or respond to a government request.
  • To any other third party with your prior consent to do so.
06 — Data Security

How we protect your information

We use reasonable and appropriate physical, technical, and administrative industry safeguards to protect your information from loss, misuse, unauthorized access or disclosure, and alteration.

A note on security. Notwithstanding our security safeguards, it is impossible to guarantee 100% security in all circumstances. We will retain and use your personal information as necessary to comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, enforce our agreements, and as reasonably useful for our organizational purposes.

07 — Third-Party Content

Seals, buttons, and content from other companies

Our website may include seals, buttons, and content links to other companies — for example, Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter, YouTube, and PayPal. These features may collect your IP address, the page you are visiting on our site, and may set a cookie to enable the feature to function properly.

We do not control these third-party websites and their associated cookies. When you use a link to navigate from globalhealthaction.org to another website, our Privacy Notice is no longer in effect. We encourage you to read the Privacy Notice of each website you visit.

08 — Policy Changes

Changes to this Privacy Notice

We may periodically update this Privacy Notice. If we make material changes, the amended version will be promptly posted on our website. Your continued use of our website after such changes will mean that you accept them. We encourage you to review this Privacy Notice each time you visit the website.
